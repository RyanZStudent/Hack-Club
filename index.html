Best Places to Spend Christams In Canada!

Here's a list of the top 4 places to spend Christmas

1. Québec City, Québec

Québec City is the perfect place to spend Christmas. There are tons of things to do and delicious food to taste. Québec’s Old Town decorates the cobblestone streets with festive lights and gorgeous decorations creating a spectacular view.

Visiting Québec City during the Christmas season offers the incredible experience of its German Christmas Market. With beautifully decorated wooden stalls, lively carolers, delicious food, and the delightful Glühwein, the Marché de Noël allemand de Québec is a must-see event.

If you're seeking more outdoor fun, Québec is a prime destination. Located just a short drive from the city, you'll discover four premier ski resorts: Mont-Sainte-Anne, Massif de Charlevoix, Stoneham, and Le Relais. These resorts offer some of the best slopes and facilities in the province.

2. Banff, Alberta

Experiencing Christmas in Banff is enchanting. Beyond the pristine ski runs, there are numerous winter activities to dive into. You can go dog sledding in Canmore, glide across the ice on Lake Louise, or enjoy a picturesque sleigh ride by the lake. And if you find yourself without the necessary gear, the rental shop inside the Chateau offers everything from skates and snowshoes to snowboards and ski equipment. This ensures that everyone can fully enjoy the winter wonderland Banff has to offer.

3. Whistler, British Columbia

Perched in the majestic mountains a mere two hours north of Vancouver, Whistler-Blackcomb stands as Canada’s top ski resort. While this destination offers incredible biking and hiking during the summer and world-class snow sports throughout the winter, it truly comes alive at Christmas. The festive season is the perfect time to experience its magical charm and unrivaled beauty.

During the holiday season, Whistler Village turns into a magical winter paradise. The charming European-style village is adorned with over 350,000 twinkling fairy lights, creating a festive and enchanting atmosphere. With frequent snowfall in December, the village often looks like a real-life snow globe. Beyond the world-class skiing and snowboarding, Whistler offers an array of delightful Christmas activities. You can enjoy a horse-drawn sleigh ride through the snowy landscape, glide on ice skates in the village, embark on an exhilarating dog sledding adventure, go tubing down snowy slopes, or explore the winter wonderland on snowshoes.

It’s a perfect destination to immerse yourself in the holiday spirit and winter fun.

4. Calgary, Alberta

The holiday season is an ideal time to explore Calgary, the jewel of western Canada. Calgary comes alive with festive events, including vibrant markets, stunning light displays, and family-friendly attractions. The city embraces its winter charm and transforms into a magical wonderland during Christmas. ZOOLIGHTS at the Wilder Institute/Calgary Zoo features over a million lights across 250 dazzling displays in the evenings.

Calgary’s Christmas markets in November and December showcase vendors selling locally made and handmade items. Notable markets include the Inglewood Night Market, Market Collective, and Spruce Meadows International Christmas Market.
